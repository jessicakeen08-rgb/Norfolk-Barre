What is barre?

STRENGTH
TRAINING
with a difference

Barre is a full-body workout that blends ballet-inspired movements with pilates and functional exercises. Don't let the name fool you. This is not a dance class, and you don't need any dance experience whatsoever.

Small, precise movements combined with high repetitions and light weights will build full body strength, increase your mobility and improve your balance. Low impact means it is kind on your joints, but your muscles will absolutely know they have worked. Will you say yes to the barre burn?

01

Full body strength

Arms, core, thighs and seat. Every muscle group in every session.

02

Low impact

Kind on your joints, hard on your muscles. Accessible at every fitness level.

03

Precision movements

Small, deliberate and isometric. The technique is exactly what makes it work.

04

Posture and core

Better posture and a stronger core are two of the first things people notice.

About me

I'm not a dancer. I'm not particularly flexible. When I first tried barre, I had no idea what I was doing and felt like a bit of a wally during the class. But I had SUCH a good workout, I booked myself straight back in again! Barre became the 50 minute slice of space and mindful movement that kept me sane, during my intense London days!

For nine years I have made barre part of my weekly movement routine and I can't live without it. It helps calm my mind, energise my body and refresh my nervous system. I love it and I hope you do too.

Since moving to Norfolk in 2020 I've practised online and regularly go down to London to visit my favourite studios, because nothing local existed. That felt like a gap worth filling.

Now qualified as a 100-hour Barre Instructor through Barre Series, I'm bringing the classes I love to Holt. If you're curious, excited to try something different, or looking to get back into barre, you are so welcome!
